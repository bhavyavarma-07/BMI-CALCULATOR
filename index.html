What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement of a person's weight with respect to their height. It is a screening tool that can indicate whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. BMI is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI of 30 or greater

Note: BMI is a general guideline and does not account for factors such as muscle mass, bone density, and overall body composition.
